AAS 330 FInal Take Aways

TAKEAWAY 1 — Immigrant’s Journey to the American Dream Is Lonely

Quote: “I don’t belong anywhere, and I don’t belong in this society. I’m all alone” (The Lonely Water Buffalo).

My Reflection: When I came to the USA in fifth grade, I felt very alone. People laughed at my accent, and I couldn’t speak English at all. I really felt like I didn’t belong here. That is why this quote feels exactly like what I went through. In AAS 330, many stories and plays talk about immigrant families dealing with pain, suffering, and losing things, just like Ma’s family. These things add to the loneliness. For me, the first two years in America were really hard. I had no friends, and I brought books because I knew they would keep me company.

TAKEAWAY 2 — It Is Hard for Immigrants to Balance Between Two Identities

Quote: “I am Lee Sung Wei, not this Moy Fook Sing or whatever his name is! I am from Shekki not Sunning. I am a scholar, not a merchant’s son!” (Lim 14).

My Reflection: When I came to the USA, I always had to hide some part of myself. With my Chinese family friends, I hid my American side. With my American friends, I hid my Chinese side. Even when choosing food, deep down I wanted Asian food, but because my American friends didn’t like those foods, I said “let’s go to Dick’s Burgers.” I did the same thing the other way around with my Asian friends.

Lee’s line made me think about how he had to pretend to be someone he wasn’t. Even though my situation is different from his, I understand the feeling of trying to fit into a new place and worrying about how people see you. That is why this takeaway matters to me.

TAKEAWAY 3 — Plays Reveal Emotion Better Than Plain Text

Quote: “Emiko still holds the empty can. Offstage we hear Oka’s car start off. Accustomed to crying alone, she doesn’t utter a sound” (Yamauchi play 202).

My Reflection: When me and my partner Jenny Chrin performed our scene, I understood how plays show emotion in a stronger way than plain text. When I performed Our Time in Linda Vista, I could act out Ma’s emotion. When the family went back to their house that was demolished, I could show how sorry and helpless they felt while picking up things quickly and leaving.

In the play And the Soul Shall Dance, the staging made Emiko’s loneliness feel much stronger. The silence and her body language made her sadness more real. In the text version, we only read things like “They’ve left their home again Mrs. Oka is alone again” (Yamauchi 23), but we don’t feel the same emotion. The play lets us see the fights, the dancing, and the silence. For me, the play version feels more alive, and the text version feels more quiet. Both show the pain of immigrants, but in different ways.
